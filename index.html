Caveats

  • Sometimes one big complex invariant beats many small ones if the big invariant is hard for humans and captures the real safety goal.
  • Sometimes many small invariants are better when they give sensible guarantees that are hard to audit manually, like access control or governance.
  • Prover value depends on model fidelity; wrong assumptions can make a proof less useful than a focused manual review.
  • Prioritize properties with stable specs and actionable counterexamples; volatile designs burn FV time.

Conclusion

Three Lessons from the Engagement

01 Local correctness is not enough

Hooks, donations, and downstream protocols can turn acceptable local behavior into material ecosystem risk.

02 Trustworthy guarantees start with precise proof boundaries

State exactly which guarantees survive the loop bound, arithmetic summaries, and ghost models, and which remain outside the proof.

03 Exhaustiveness still needs human judgment

Manual reasoning finds semantic and modeling gaps; formal verification states and explores the resulting claims precisely.
